Team's Rationale

Methodology

Planning Phase

  • Get the opportunity to know the client
    • Find out information about what are their goals, challenges, vision about their project.
    • Gather information about what the client does today in their current business.
    • Break information down into tasks.
    • Understand the “why” behind every task.
  • Assess the priorities of the tasks.
  • Determine how much the basic time and effort needed to complete tasks.
  • Negotiate the budget and the cost for completing the project.

Design Phase

  • Discuss with team the fine details of each tasks.
  • Create prototypes and mock-ups (using various tools like Photoshop, paper prototyping, etc) and apply feedback from development team.

Post-Design Feedback Phase

  • Meet with the client to show the initial mock ups and designs.
  • Discuss what works and what does not in terms of what was proposed in Planning Phase.
  • If tasks still require further work we go back to Design Phase.
  • Else if we get the approval or the go-ahead, we move to Development Phase.

Development Phase

  • Team will work on building the main tasks for a more finished version. We will use our skill set to build and test.
  • Team will provide feedback and do reviews before moving to next phase.

Post-Development Feedback Phase

  • Meet with the client to show the more finished version (Alpha, Beta, etc).
  • Discuss what works and what does not in terms of what was proposed in Design Phase.
  • If tasks still require further work we go back to Development Phase.
  • Else if we get the approval or the go-ahead, we finish off the remainder of our tasks and move to Delivery Phase.

Delivery Phase

  • Launch the project.
  • Provide maintenance.
  • Provide tutorials for the client to let them operate on certain content management of the site.
  • Go out for a beer to celebrate.
